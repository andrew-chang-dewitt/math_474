## Problem 1

:::{.question}

> (Exercise 5.13.8). An article in Electric Power Systems Research
> ["Modeling Real-Time Balancing Power Demands in Wind Power Systems Using Stochastic Differential Equations" (2010, Vol. 80(8), pp. 966–974)]
> considered a new probabilistic model to balance power demand with large
> amounts of wind power. In this model, the power loss from shutdowns is
> assumed to have a triangular distribution with probability density function
>
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> f(x) &= \begin{cases}
>           -5.56 \times 10^{-4} + 5.56 \times 10^{-6}x,
>               &100 \le x \le 500, \\
>           4.44 \times 10^{-3} + -4.44 \times 10^{-6}x,
>               &500 <   x \le 1000, \\
>           0,  &x \not \in [100,1000] \\
>         \end{cases} \tag{1.1}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>
> Determine the following:

:::

cdf, from (1.1):

$$
\begin{align}
F(X = x) &= \int_{-\infty}^x f(u)du \notag \\
F(X = x) &= \begin{cases}
          0,  &x < 100, \\
          \int_{100}^x \big[-5.56 \times 10^{-4} + 5.56 \times 10^{-6}u\big]du,
              &100 \le x \le 500, \\
          F(X = 500) + \int_{500}^x \big[4.44 \times 10^{-3} - 4.44 \times 10^{-6}u\big]du,
              &500 <   x \le 1000, \\
          1,  &otherwise \\
        \end{cases} \notag \\
         &= \begin{cases}
          0,  &x < 100, \\
          \big[-5.56 \times 10^{-4} u + 5.56 \times 10^{-6} \frac{1}{2} u^2\big]_{100}^x,
              &100 \le x \le 500, \\
          F(X = 500) + \big[4.44 \times 10^{-3} u - 4.44 \times 10^{-6} \frac{1}{2} u^2\big]_{500}^x,
              &500 <   x \le 1000, \\
          1,  &otherwise \\
        \end{cases} \notag \\
         &= \begin{cases}
          0,  &x < 100, \\
          \big[-5.56 \times 10^{-4} x + 5.56 \times 10^{-6} \frac{1}{2} x^2\big] \\
          \quad - \big[-5.56 \times 10^{-4} (100) + 5.56 \times 10^{-6} \frac{1}{2} (100)^2\big],
              &100 \le x \le 500, \\
          F(X = 500) \\
          \quad + \big[4.44 \times 10^{-3} x - 4.44 \times 10^{-6} \frac{1}{2} x^2\big] \\
          \quad - \big[4.44 \times 10^{-3} (500) - 4.44 \times 10^{-6} \frac{1}{2} (500)^2\big],
              &500 <   x \le 1000, \\
          1,  &otherwise \\
        \end{cases} \notag \\
F(X = x) &= \begin{cases}
          0,  &x < 100, \\
          2.78 \times 10^{-6} x^2 - 5.56 \times 10^{-4} x + 0.0278,
              &100 \le x \le 500, \\
          -2.22 \times 10^{-6} x^2 + 4.44 \times 10^{-3} x - 1.2202,
              &500 <   x \le 1000, \\
          1,  &otherwise \\
        \end{cases} \tag{1.2}
\end{align}
$$

<section>

### part (a)

:::{.question}

> P(X < 500).

:::

$$
\begin{align}
P(X < 500) &= F(500) \notag \\
           &= -2.22 \times 10^{-6} (500)^2 + 4.44 \times 10^{-3} (500) - 1.2202 &&
               \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// case $100 \le x \le 500$ of (1.2)}} \notag \\
           &= 0.4448 \space_\blacksquare \tag{1.a}
\end{align}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (b)

:::{.question}

> P(100 < X ≤ 200).

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
P(100 < X ≤ 200)
    &= F(200) - F(100) \\
    &= F(200) &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// $F(100) = 0$}} \\
    &= -2.22 \times 10^{-6} (100)^2 + 4.44 \times 10^{-3} (100) - 1.2202 &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// case $100 \le x \le 500$ of (1.2)}} \\
    &= 0.0277\bar{9} \space_\blacksquare
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (c)

:::{.question}

> P(X > 800)

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
P(X > 800)
    &= 1 - P(X \le 800) \\
    &= 1 - F(800) \\
    &= 1 - (-2.22 \times 10^{-6} x^2 + 4.44 \times 10^{-3} x - 1.2202) &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// case $500 \lt x \le 1000$ of (1.2)}} \\
    &= 1 - 0.911 \\
    &= 0.089 \space_\blacksquare
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (d)

:::{.question}

> Find the power loss value such that only 10% of power losses are
> larger than this value.

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
P(X > x) &= 0.10 = 1 - P(X \le x) \\
    0.10 &= 1 - F(x) \\
    0.90 &= F(x) &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// 0.90 > F(500), by (1.a)}} \\
    0.90 &= -2.22 \times 10^{-6} x^2 + 4.44 \times 10^{-3} x - 1.2202 &&

        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by quadratic formula}} \\
\end{aligned}
$$

## Problem 2

:::{.question}

> (Exercise 5.13.15). The probability density function of the time you
> arrive at a terminal (in minutes after 8:00 A.M.) is f(x) = 0.1e:sup[−0.1x]
> for 0 < x. Determine the probability that

:::

first, let's define that pmf piecewise:

$$
\begin{align}
f(x) &= \begin{cases}
          \frac{1}{10}e^{−\frac{1}{10}x}, x > 0 \\
          0,            &otherwise \\
        \end{cases} \tag{2.1}
\end{align}
$$

then derive a cdf from it:

$$
\begin{align}
F(X = x) &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x < 0, \\
              \int_0^x \big[\frac{1}{10}e^{−\frac{1}{10}u}\big] du,
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \notag \\
         &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x < 0, \\
              \big[-e^{−\frac{1}{10}u}\big]_0^x,
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \notag \\
         &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x < 0, \\
              \big[-e^{−\frac{1}{10}x}\big] - \big[-e^{−\frac{1}{10}(0)}\big],
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \notag \\
F(X = x) &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x < 0, \\
              \big[-e^{−\frac{1}{10}x}\big] + 1,
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \tag{2.2}
\end{align}
$$

<section>

### part (a)

:::{.question}

> You arrive by 9:00 A.M.

:::

by 9:00 A.M. is 60 minutes later, so

$$
\begin{aligned}
P(X < 60)
    &= F(60) \\
    &= \big[-e^{−6}\big] + 1 &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by (2.2)}} \\
    &approx 0.9975 \space_\blacksquare
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (b)

:::{.question}

> You arrive between 8:15 A.M. and 8:30 A.M.

:::

$$
\begin{align}
P(15 < X < 30)
    &= P(X < 30) - P(X > 15) = F(30) - F(15)\notag \\
    &= \big[-e^{−3}\big] + 1 - \big[-e^{−\frac{5}{3}x}\big] - 1 &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by (2.2)}}\notag \\
    &= \big[-e^{−3}\big] - \big[-e^{−\frac{5}{3}x}\big]\notag \\
    &\approx 0.9502 - 0.7769\notag \\

## Problem 3

:::{.question}

> (Exercise 5.13.26). The thickness of a conductive coating (in
> micrometers) has a density function of f(x) = 600x:sup[−2] for 100 < x < 120.

:::

pmf & cdf of $X$, a rand var representing thickness of conductive coating:

$$
\begin{align}
f(x) &= \begin{cases}
          600x^{−2}, &100 < x < 120 \\
          0, &otherwise \\
        \end{cases} \tag{3.1} \\
\notag \\
F(X = x) &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x \le 100 \\
              \int_{100}^x 600u^{-2} du,
                  &100 < x < 120, \\
              1,
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \notag \\
         &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x \le 100 \\
              600[-x^-1]_{100}^x,
                  &100 < x < 120, \\
              1,
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \notag \\
F(X = x) &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x \le 100 \\
              600[-\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{100}],
                  &100 < x < 120, \\
              1,
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \notag \\
F(X = x) &= \begin{cases}
              0,
                  &x \le 100 \\
              6 -\frac{600}{x},
                  &100 < x < 120, \\
              1,
                  &otherwise \\
            \end{cases} \tag{3.2} \\
\end{align}
$$

<section>

### part (a)

:::{.question}

> If the coating costs $0.50 per micrometer of thickness on each part,
> what is the average cost of the coating per part?

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
            h(x) &= 0.50x \\
\mathbb{E}[h(X)] &= \int_{100}^{120} h(x) f(x) dx &&
    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by def of mean for cont. R.V.}} \\
                 &= \int_{100}^{120} \$0.5x 600x^{-2} dx \\
                 &= 300 \int_{100}^{120} x^{-1} dx \\
                 &= 300 [ln x]_{100}^{120} \\
                 &= 300 [ln(120) - ln(100)] \\
                 &= \$54.70 \space_\blacksquare
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (b)

:::{.question}

> Determine the mean and variance of the coating thickness.

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mu &= \mathbb{E}[X] \\
    &= \int_{100}^{120} x f(x) dx &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by def of mean for cont. R.V.}} \\
    &= \int_{100}^{120} x 600x^{-2} dx \\
    &= 600 \int_{100}^{120} x^{-1} dx \\
    &= 600 [ln x]_{100}^{120} \\
    &= 300 [ln(120) - ln(100)] \\
    &= 109.4 \text{ $\mu$m} \space_\blacksquare \\
\\
\sigma^2 &= \text{Var}(X) \\
         &= \int_{100}^{120} x^2 f(x) dx - \mu^2 \\
         &= \int_{100}^{120} x^2 600x^{-2} dx - \mu^2 \\
         &= 600 \int_{100}^{120} dx - \mu^2 \\
         &= 600 (20) - 109.4^2 \\
         &= 31.64 \text{ $\mu$m}^2 \space_\blacksquare
\end{aligned}
$$

## Problem 4

> (Exercise 5.13.34, selected parts). An electron emitter produces
> electron beams with changing kinetic energy that is uniformly
> distributed between 3 and 7 joules. Suppose that it is possible to
> adjust the upper limit of the kinetic energy (currently set to 7
> joules).

:::

<section>

### part (a)

:::{.question}

> What is the mean and variance of the kinetic energy?

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mu &= \frac{a + b}{2} &&
        \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// mean of uniform continuous RV}} \\
    &= \frac{3 + 7}{2} \\
    &= 5 \text{ joules} \space_\blacksquare \\
\\
\sigma^2 &= \frac{(b - a)^2}{12} &&
             \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// var of uniform cont. RV}} \\
         &=  \frac{16}{12} \\
         &=  \frac{4}{3} \text{ joules}^2 \space_\blacksquare \\
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (b)

:::{.question}

> What is the probability that an electron beam has a kinetic energy of
> exactly 3.2 joules?

:::

The probability is 0 because kinetic energy is modeled as a continous uniform random variable. To determine the probability of any continuous random variable an _interval_ must be considered, never an _exact_ value.

</section>
<section>

### part (c)

:::{.question}

> What should be the upper limit so that the variance of kinetic energy
> decreases to 0.75 joules:sup[2]?

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
\sigma^2 &= 0.75 \text{ joules}^2 \\
    0.75 &= \frac{(b - 3)^2}{12} \\
       9 &= b^2 - 6b + 9 \\
       0 &= b(b - 6) \\
       b &= 6 \text{ joules} \space_\blacksquare \\
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>

## Problem 5

:::{.question}

> (Exercise 5.13.42, selected parts). Cholesterol is a fatty substance
> that is an important part of the outer lining (membrane) of cells in
> the body of animals. Its normal range for an adult is 125-200 mg/dL.
> The Food and Nutrition Institute of the Philip pines found that the
> total cholesterol level for Filipino adults has a mean of 159.2 mg/dL
> and 84.15% of adults have a cholesterol level less than 200 mg/dL.
> Suppose that the total cholesterol level is normally distributed.

:::

some values from the text:

$$
\begin{aligned}
         X &\coloneqq \text{ cholesterol level as norm. dist. RV } \\
       \mu &= 159.2 \text{ mg/dL } \\
P(X < 200) &= 0.8415 \\
\end{aligned}
$$

<section>

### part (a)

:::{.question}

> Determine the standard deviation of this distribution.

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
         &P(X \text{ w/in 1 $\sigma$ of $\mu$}) = 0.6827 \\
\implies &P(X < (\sigma + \mu)) = \frac{1 - 0.6827}{2} + 0.6827 = 0.84135 \\
         &P(X < (\sigma + \mu)) \approx P(X < 200) \\
\implies &X < (\sigma + \mu) \approx X < 200 \\
\implies &\sigma + \mu = 200 \\
\implies &\sigma = 200 - \mu = 40.8 \text{ mg/dL} \space_\blacksquare \\
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (b)

:::{.question}

> What are the quartiles (the 25% and 75% percentiles) of this
> distribution?

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
         &P_l(Z \le z_l) = 0.25 &&
    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// first quartile}} \\
\implies &z_l = -0.67 &&
    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by z-table}} \\
\implies &\frac{x_l - \mu}{\sigma} = -0.67 \\
         &x_l - \mu = -0.67 \sigma \\
         &x_l = -0.67 \sigma + \mu \\
         &x_l = 131.864 \text{ mg/dL} \space_\blacksquare \\
\\
         &P_h(Z \le z_h) = 0.75 &&
    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// third quartile}} \\
\implies &z_h = 0.68 &&
    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by z-table}} \\
\implies &\frac{x_h - \mu}{\sigma} = 0.68 \\
         &x_h - \mu = 0.68 \sigma \\
         &x_h = 0.68 \sigma + \mu \\
         &x_h = 186.944 \text{ mg/dL} \space_\blacksquare \\
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (c)

:::{.question}

> Find the cholesterol level such that 90% of adults have total
> cholesterol at or below this level.

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
         &P_{90}(Z \le z_{90}) = 0.90 &&
    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// first quartile}} \\
\implies &z_{90} = 1.29 &&
    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by z-table}} \\
\implies &\frac{x_{90} - \mu}{\sigma} = 1.29 \\
         &x_{90} - \mu = 1.29 \sigma \\
         &x_{90} = 1.29 \sigma + \mu \\
         &x_{90} = 211.832 \text{ mg/dL} \space_\blacksquare \\
\end{aligned}
$$

</section>
<section>

### part (d)

:::{.question}

> An adult is at moderate risk if cholesterol level is more than 1 but

P(X > 2\sigma) &= 1 - P(X < 2\sigma) \\

    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// by z-table}} \\

    \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{// 2.275\% of the population is at risk}} \\
\end{aligned}
$$
