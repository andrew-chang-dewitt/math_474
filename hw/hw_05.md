## Problem 1

:::{.question}

> **(Exercise 4.10.10 and 4.10.11 (selected parts))**. Verify that the
> following functions are probability mass functions:

:::

recall the definition of **pmf**:

"for a function $f: X \mapsto \real$, it can be considered to be a
probability mass function if the following criteria are satisified for all $x \in X$":

$$
\begin{align}
& f(x) \ge 0                   \tag{1.1} \\
& \sum_{x \in X} f(x) = 1      \tag{1.2} \\
& f(x) = \mathbb{P}(X = x)     \tag{1.3} \\
\end{align}
$$

with this, we can define the proposition `isPMF(f)` as:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{isPMF}(f) &\iff \forall x \in X \;[ (1.1) \land (1.2) \land (1.3) ] \tag{1.4}\\
\end{aligned}
$$

<section>

### part (a)

:::{.question}

> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> f(x) = \frac{8}{7}\bigg(\frac{1}{2}\bigg)^x, x = 1,2,3 \tag{1.a}
> \end{aligned}
> $$

:::

$$
\begin{aligned}
\because
  && f(1) &= \frac{8}{7}\bigg(\frac{1}{2}\bigg)^1 = \frac{4}{7} \\
  && f(2) &= \frac{8}{7}\bigg(\frac{1}{2}\bigg)^2 = \frac{8}{7}\bigg(\frac{1}{4}\bigg) = \frac{2}{7} \\
  && f(3) &= \frac{8}{7}\bigg(\frac{1}{2}\bigg)^3 = \frac{8}{7}\bigg(\frac{1}{8}\bigg) = \frac{1}{7} \\
\\
  && A &\coloneqq \forall x \in X \; f(x) \ge 0 &&
     \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{pmf criteria (1.1)}} \\
  &&   &= [ f(1) \ge 0 ] \land [ f(2) \ge 0 ] \land [ f(3) \ge 0 ] \\
  &&   &= T \\
\\
  && B &\coloneqq \sum_{x \in X} f(x) = 1 &&
     \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{pmf criteria (1.2)}} \\
  &&   &= \bigg( \frac{4}{7} + \frac{2}{7} + \frac{1}{7} = 1 \bigg) = \bigg(\frac{7}{7} = 1\bigg) \\
  &&   &= T \\
\\
  &&   &\text{if we assume that $f(x)$ is giving} \\
  &&   &\text{probabilities of $x \in X$, then} &&
     \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{pmf criteria (1.3)}} \\
\\
\therefore
  && \text{isPMF}(f) &\iff A \land B \land T &&
     \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{by (1.4)}} \\
  && \text{isPMF}(f) &\iff T \land T \land T \\
  && \text{isPMF}(f) &\iff T \space_\blacksquare && \htmlClass{hljs-comment}{\textit{$f(x$ is a pmf)}} \\
\end{aligned}
$$
