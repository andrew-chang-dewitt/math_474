### part (a)

> Find $P(A∩B∩C)$

$$
\begin{aligned}
(8) && ∵ && P(A_1∩A_2∩…&∩A_k) \\
    &&   &&   &= P(A_1) P(A_2|A_1) P(A_3|A_1∩A_2) \\
    &&   &&   &… P(A_k|A_1∩A_2∩…∩A_{k-1}), \textit{where} \\
    &&   &&   &\quad A_1,A_2,…,A_k \text{ are events that can occur} &&
  \textit{\{theorem 2.12} \\
    &&   && & &&
  \textit{ [Walpole, Myers, Myers, Ye]\}} \\
    &&   && P(A∩B∩C) &= P(A) P(B|A) P(C|A∩B) &&
  \textit{\{8\}} \\
    &&   &&                    &= 0.3 * 0.75 * 0.20 &&
  \textit{\{1,2,4\}} \\
    && ∴ && P(A∩B∩C) &= 0.045 \quad\blacksquare
\end{aligned}
$$
