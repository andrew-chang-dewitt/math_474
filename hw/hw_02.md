#### part (d)

:::{.question}

> For any set of data values, is it possible for the sample standard deviation
> to be larger than the sample mean? If so,give an example.

:::

No. Recall the definition of standard deviation:

$$
\begin{align}
s &= \sqrt{s^2}, \\
  &where  \notag \\
  &s^2 = \frac{1}{n - 1} \sum^n_{i \coloneqq 1} (x_i - \bar{x})^2 \\
  &\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum^n_{i \coloneqq 1} x_i
\end{align}
$$

Since $\bar{x}$, the mean, is subtracted from each datapoint & the $\bar{x}$
will always be near the center of a dataset, it is impossible for $s$ to be
larger than $\bar{x}$

</section>

#### part (e)

:::{.question}

> Can the sample standard deviation be equal to zero? If so,give an example.

:::
Yes, given a dataset of 2 equal value:

$$
\begin{aligned}
      x &\coloneqq \{ 10, 10 \} \\
\\
\bar{x} &= \frac{1}{n} \sum^n_{i \coloneqq 1} x_i
        = \frac{1}{2} 20 \\
        &= 10 \\
\\
  s^2_x &= \frac{1}{n - 1} \sum^n_{i \coloneqq 1} (x_i - \bar{x})^2 \\
        &= \frac{1}{1} \big((10-10)^2 + (10-10)^2\big)
        = 1 (0) \\
        &= 0 \\
\\
    s_x &= \sqrt{s^2_x}
        = \sqrt{0} \\
        &= 0 \space_\blacksquare \\
\end{aligned}
$$
